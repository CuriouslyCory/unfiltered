# Analysis of Executive Grant of Clemency for TERENCE SUTTON

This is an Executive Grant of Clemency signed by Donald J. Trump on January 22, 2025, that grants a full and unconditional pardon to Terence Sutton for his convictions in the United States District Court for the District of Columbia.

## Key Points

- **Grant of Clemency:** Donald J. Trump, President of the United States, has granted a full and unconditional pardon to Terence Sutton.
- **Convictions:** Sutton was convicted in the United States District Court for the District of Columbia on violations of section 2103, title 22, District of Columbia Code; section 371, title 18, United States Code; and section 1512, title 18, United States Code.
- **Sentencing:** Sutton was sentenced on September 12, 2024, to 66, 48, and 48 months of imprisonment (all to run concurrently), 3 years of supervised release, and a special assessment of $300.
- **Pardon Attorney:** The Pardon Attorney is designated as the President's representative to sign the grant of clemency.

- **No Explicit Justification:** The document does not provide specific reasoning or justification for granting the pardon. This can be a concern if the decision appears arbitrary or if there are public policy reasons why the pardon may be viewed negatively.

## Constitutional Considerations

- **Presidential Power to Pardon:** Article II, Section 2, Clause 1 of the Constitution grants the President the power to grant pardons for offenses against the United States, except in cases of impeachment.
- **No Checks and Balances:**  Presidential pardons are largely unchecked, meaning that the decision is made at the sole discretion of the President.

**Potential Implications:**
