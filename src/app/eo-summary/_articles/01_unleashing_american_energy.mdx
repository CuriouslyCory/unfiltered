# Analysis of Executive Orderâ€”Unleashing American Energy

This Executive Order, signed on January 20, 2025, aims to promote energy independence by encouraging exploration and production, streamlining the permitting process, and reversing several prior executive actions and environmental policies.

## Key Points

-   **Policy Focus**: Encourages energy exploration and production on federal lands and waters, including the Outer Continental Shelf.
-   **Mineral Dominance**: Seeks to establish the U.S. as a leading producer of non-fuel minerals, including rare earth minerals.
-   **Regulatory Review**: Mandates review of existing regulations that may burden domestic energy resources.
-   **Consumer Choice**: Aims to eliminate EV mandates and promote consumer choice in vehicles and appliances.
-   **Permitting Efficiency**: Revokes Executive Order 11991, seeks to expedite the permitting process, and prioritize efficiency in environmental analysis.
-   **Social Cost of Carbon**: Disbands the Interagency Working Group on the Social Cost of Greenhouse Gases and aims to eliminate the use of "social cost of carbon" calculations.
-   **Green New Deal Termination**: Pauses the disbursement of funds from the Inflation Reduction Act and Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act.
-   **LNG Exports**: Directs the Secretary of Energy to expedite reviews of liquified natural gas export projects.
-   **Mineral Dominance**: Seeks to restore America's mineral dominance by identifying and revising actions that impose burdens on mining and processing of non-fuel minerals.

## Areas of Concern

-   **Environmental Regulations**: The executive order seeks to weaken or eliminate regulations aimed at protecting the environment. The order revokes multiple executive orders focusing on environmental protection and climate change mitigation.
